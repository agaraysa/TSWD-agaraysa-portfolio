# Global Average Debt-to-GDP Ratio Comparison 
_This was truly my first time using Tableau, and I was having a hard time achieving my ultimate vision, so although this data visualization is not perfect, I will also speak on how it can be improved.
First and foremost, the goal of my line graph was to serve as an interactive dashboard that allows each country to be compared to the global average Debt-to-GDP Ratio by highlighting the line. As suggested by the readings, adding a label at the end of these lines makes it much more pleasing to the eye and limits eye movement within the visualization, allowing the information to be registered more effectively. However, since I included all the countries, the labels are cluttered and overlap. To reduce visual clutter, I greyed out the text and shortened the countries’ names to three letters; however, it is still not the best depiction of the country names. I also made sure to gray out the other lines to ensure the global average stood out, and when using the interactive piece, to allow the selected lines to clearly show up against the rest. I think my grey is a bit col, and should have mad it more of a “warm grey” as noted in the reading to make it less cold  I had hoped for the global average to stay fixed and serve as an anchor where selecting the other lines would highlight them in intuitive colors that stray negatively (higher debt to GDP ratio depicted as red), close to the average (depicted as yellow), or positively away from the global average (depicted as green). Some intermediary lines can appear orange to indicate whether they are getting closer to or further from the global average. I would have also liked to see this depicted as a gradient when not using the dashboard in this more direct way, but after one interacts with it. My audience would include someone looking to understand how their country, or others, compares to the global average, while also understanding where the rest of the world stands.
Some of the main things I would have changed were to remove the title on my x-axis because it is redundant, and to clarify my y-axis label, since it does not convey the magnitude of the numbers displayed. However, I did not include this because I was also unsure of that magnitude.  I would update that header to include and cite the OECD general government debt data used, since I did not include it._
