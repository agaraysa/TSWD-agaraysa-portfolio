# About me
Hello! My name is Ale, and my pronouns are she/her. I grew up in a border town between Mexico and the United States that allowed me to have the privilege to be submerged in a bicultural and bilingual way of life. Spanish was my first language but I learned English once I began kinder. I have always been proud of my cultural background and always ensure that I integrate identity in everything I do, whether it is at work or when meeting new people. My passion is in advocating for community members that do not have a voice, advocating for issues that support the environment or social justice issues. The journey that made me realize I wanted to pursue a career in public policy was one that took many directions before arriving at my masters degree. As an undergraduate student, I decided to major in pre-veterinary sciences, thinking my path was to become a veterinarian but I eventually realized that was not my calling. Instead, I had discovered my love for environmental conservation when minoring in marine sciences. I explored doing on the ground research through doing native AZ fish surveys, in addition to other types of native AZ critters like prairie dogs, that took me to some beautiful hikes and areas in the Sonoran Desert. But again that was not my calling. It wasn't until I took a course that gave me the opportunity to learn about the Conservation realities of Northern Mexico that made me realize the complexities of policies and their impact on the environment, it's rural communities that are the ones that suffer the major impacts of climate change and or reduced access or disruption to natural resources that disrupts their subsistence way of living off the land. That experience, in addition to other career experiences that came after that, allowed me to lead and work with various types of people and teams doing environmental restoration work, that made me realize I wanted to be a part of advocating for others and the environment.

When I graduate, I hope to get a job that I love and am able to make a difference! I am looking for a government relations position that will allow me to work in legislation to advocate for policies and legilsation that advocates for important issues. Additionally, I hope to gain experience in the realm lobbying for social justice and enviromental causes.
