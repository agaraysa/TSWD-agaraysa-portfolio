## Changes made since Part II & Final Design Decisions

_After Part II feedback, I decided to add a section to cover an example of a real life RCO by using the organization I intern at, SHUC, as an example to show how the RCO designation benefits the community, and added some simple "call-out" stats by bolding certain numbers: the amount of displaced residents affected by a local apartment fire that were able to get resources, and the amount of development meetings they organized in 2025._ 

_After my presentation, I made some aesthetic changes and incorporated feedback from my classmates, which I found really helpful in making my project's final product more complete and cohesive. The only thing I did not change was my “call-out stats,” since I liked how the template flowed, and I could not figure out how to add a smaller callout stat within a section instead of creating a separate section that uses the shorthand template. Since creating an entirely new section would mess with my flow, I decided to leave it as is. Next, I integrated a more visually appealing template for the maps visualization section since the one I had before made it look clunky and did not blend in aesthetically. I also added some more text here to provide some context, but kept it simple. In my last section, my call-to-action, I added more community resources specific to folks being able to connect/find out who their RCO is, and if not contact information of their council member so that they can write to their district's council member to support in the process to mobilize an already established community group that is a non-profit to apply for RCO designation and/or to help organizing a community group that can receive the required non-profit designation necessary to qualify to become an RCO. I also added a link that explains the requirements for starting the RCO process. Lastly, I added more text/paragraphs to provide more context and understanding of the materials, to help guide someone who would be browsing my story on their own time, so that it is a standalone story, and a call to action can be understood clearly if they happen to be just by scrolling through it._

## The audience

_My goal in the end was to raise awareness of the benefits of Registered Community Organizations, to inform community members and administration/leadership from any community group about those benefits, and to learn how to apply. Additionally, one of my main goals is to highlight that about half of the City of Pittsburgh's neighborhoods still lack an RCO, so that community members and/or community groups can find out and have a "one-stop shop" to know where to begin this process. This insight and the narrowing of what the story was and for whom it should be, thanks to my interviewers, my in-class critique team during in-class critique sessions, Part II feedback, and the feedback I received from my classmates after my presentation! The in-class presentation helped me figure out how to build my call-to-action section. Their interpretation of who they felt I was targeting made me realize that my audience can be any community member who may see it and pique their curiosity about what RCO presence is in their neighborhood and whether they have that representation._


## Final thoughts and reflection of my final project

_I was initially struggling to pinpoint my "why" and "what" my story was. I had the inspiration for the community organization that I have been interning for the past two academic years as a master's student (SHUC). However, it was difficult to flesh out, and I really do owe it to my in-class critique group, to the interviews with friends and family during this process, for helping me identify that I had no actual interesting story, to then be able to get to a point to build more of a story arc that can connect my RCO subject focus to a meaningful story._

_You can see my story develop and become more specific as you move from Part I to Part II, and now in Part III, again guided by the feedback I received from my classmates and feedback from the Part II assignment, which helped with condensing my visualizations, and understand what was working and what wasn't (and also acknowledging that if I wanted to I can incorporate other visualizations depicting meaningful data that can be added to the main story if I want to highlight even further the importance for those neighborhoods, that do not have that representation, and further emphasize that they are priority in establishing their RCO. Specifically, include data visualizations that depict and compare median income across these neighborhoods and the presence or absence of an RCO. Including these would further connect with my overall story and increase its impact by raising awareness and expanding access to resources. Kudos to one of my interviewers, who really helped me iterate on my story and gave me specific ideas for better highlighting what I should focus on in my map visualizations. They were the ones who noted that, visually, the neighborhoods without RCOs were not being highlighted, which was what I was focusing on when describing my “why” to them. Additionally, they helped me cut some of the earlier visualizations described in Part I that did not fit my story and would have simply taken up space. I was trying to make it fit the data visualization criteria for this project, but I wasn't intentional about whether it flowed with my story, and I totally missed the “creating a story with data” piece of this class. This process really helped me identify the “so what” piece._

_In retrospect the experience this class was able to put me through, was a great reminder and mental exercise (from the creative side of my brain that had been neglected) of creativity and storytelling through means of art, and it being done in a quantitative way but only to complement not necessarily that data has to take the lead always, but how it can also be used like the elements of color by visually incorporating it in an aesthetic and appealing way. So, shout out to this course on bringing that creativity out that I now realize I need to nurture and grow, thank you! Also, to my friend who was a key interviewer who helped me make intentional cuts and find the final story, you know who you are!_
